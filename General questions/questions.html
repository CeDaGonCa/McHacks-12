Waiting time

How do ER estimated wait times work?
Emergency room wait times can vary and may not always be exact. Patients are seen based on the severity of their injury or illness, with critical cases prioritized first. This ensures those in the most urgent need receive immediate care. Thank you for your understanding and patience.
How much time do I have to wait?
You can check your estimated wait time by visiting our website and using your ID to log in. If you feel your symptoms have worsened, please report the changes through the website, and a healthcare professional will reassess your case. Thank you for your cooperation.

Relaxation Techniques

How can I calm down if I feel anxious?
Try deep breathing exercises: inhale slowly for 4 seconds, hold for 4 seconds, and exhale for 4 seconds. Repeat until you feel calmer.
Breathing exercises to reduce stress in the ER.
Sit comfortably, close your eyes, and focus on your breath. Count to 4 as you inhale, hold for 4 seconds, and exhale for 4 seconds. Repeat.
Simple stretches to relieve physical tension.
Stretch your arms overhead, rotate your shoulders, and gently twist your torso from side to side to relieve tension.

How to Manage a Wound

What should I do if my wound is bleeding?
Apply gentle pressure to the wound using a clean cloth or bandage for 10-15 minutes. If bleeding persists, seek immediate medical attention.
How to clean my wound at home?
Rinse the wound gently with clean water. Use a mild soap to clean the area around the wound. Avoid scrubbing the wound directly.
When should I change the dressing?
Change the dressing daily or whenever it becomes wet or dirty. Ensure the wound is clean and dry before applying a new dressing.
How do I know if my wound is infected?
Signs of infection include redness, swelling, warmth, pain, or pus discharge. If you notice any of these, consult a healthcare provider.
Can I get my wound wet in the shower?
Keep your wound dry while showering. Use a waterproof cover if necessary.
What should I do if the wound reopens?
Reapply pressure to stop the bleeding and clean the wound. Seek medical advice if the wound does not close or becomes infected.

Dietary Restrictions

Are there foods to avoid when healing from a wound?
Avoid highly processed foods, sugary snacks, and alcohol. Focus on nutrient-rich foods to aid healing.
Should I increase my protein intake for faster recovery?
Yes, proteins like lean meats, fish, eggs, and legumes can help repair tissues and speed up recovery.
Can I drink alcohol while healing?
It's best to avoid alcohol as it can interfere with wound healing and suppress your immune system.
